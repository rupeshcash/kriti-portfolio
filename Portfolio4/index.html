PROJECT 1

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Visually impaired users face significant barriers in navigating Indian railway stations due to inadequate accessibility features and support system.
This hampers their independence and inclusivity in public transportation.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

This project aims to shed some light on one of many problems that is faced by a visually impaired people.

We aim to understand the challenges from the point of view of the visually impaired users in navigating through the Indian railway system and propose practical user-centric solutions to enhance their accessibility, safety, and overall travel experience, thereby contributing to a more inclusive society.

INSIGHTS

1. Infrastructure Gaps:
Indian Railways lacks well-maintained tactile paths, Braille signage, and adequate lighting—making navigation difficult compared to metro systems.
2. Communication Failures:
Unclear or absent announcements, especially inside trains, and no real-time updates create confusion around coach locations, station stops, and platform changes.
3. Dependence and Safety Risks:
Crowded, unmarked pathways and lack of trained staff increase reliance on others, compromising safety and independence.
4. Limited Tech Integration:
While screen readers help with booking, advanced tools like GPS-based trackers are often impractical due to cost, maintenance, and theft concerns.
5. Actionable Improvements:
Adopt metro-like features—tactile maps, clear announcements, trained helpers—and integrate secure, affordable tech solutions alongside staff training and public awareness campaigns.

PROJECT 2

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Child immunization in India is a critical public health intervention aimed at preventing life-threatening diseases. However, child immunization rate in India are below desired levels due to vaccine hesitancy, undervaccination, lack of awareness in remote areas, and poor recordkeeping of vaccines details. As a result many children remain vulnerable to preventable diseases.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Despite significant progress, child vaccination in India still faces challenges in achieving universal coverage. This project explores innovative design strategies to improve vaccine accessibility, streamline immunisation processes, and enhance outreach for better coverage.

INSIGHTS

AUTHENTIC INFORMATION SOURCE
PROBLEM: Parents rely solely on healthcare professionals for vaccine information and lack trust in other sources.
IMPACT: Limited access to diverse and authentic vaccine-related information.
USER NEEDS: Access to authentic vaccine information from reliable sources other than healthcare professionals.
CONVENIENCE IN IMMUNISATION PROCESS
PROBLEM: Parents encounter inconvenience in choosing vaccination locations and time slots according to their convenience.
IMPACT: Difficulty in finding convenient vaccination time slot and locations for smooth immunisation process for their children.
USER NEEDS: Flexibility in choosing vaccination locations and explanations about vaccine benefits in simpler terms.
APPOINTMENT SCHEDULING & BOOKING
PROBLEM: Long wait times and queues at hospitals and vaccination camps due to no system or inconvenient systems for booking appointments.
IMPACT: Long queues and waiting times cause frustration and discourage timely immunisation.
USER NEEDS: Online appointment booking system with flexible scheduling options and wait time estimates.
ACCESSIBILITY OF RECORDS & CERTIFICATES
PROBLEM: Parents struggle to access, track and manage a child's immunisation records easily since it currently available to them in physical format.
IMPACT: Parents rely on physical immunisation cards, leading to potential loss or damage. Confusion about next vaccine due date, uncertainty about complete vaccination history.
USER NEEDS: Desire for a centralised portal containing all immunisation information, accessible anytime and anywhere.
